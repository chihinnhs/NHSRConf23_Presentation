The challenges

  • Excel heavy

    • Historically consisted of 8 separate Excel based weekly reports
    • Expected to be ‘publication ready’ within a tight timescale (around 2.5 hours) 😰😰😰

  • Business continuity

    • A diverse range of stakeholders
    • Stakeholders mostly with limited exposure to R (primarily familiar with MS Office products such as Excel and PowerPoint)

The challenges (continued)

  • Technical challenges

    • Diverse geographical interests and policy driven requirements
    • Limited resource and time - no Shiny server hosting capacity (at least not yet)
    • Static PowerPoint slide style products were preferred over other innovative by some
      Limited version controls on R, R Packages and the existing R scripts - results not reproducible

The solution

  • A Rmarkdown dashboard that is interactive, reproducible and user friendly (backward compatibility)

  • Tips on how to cater to a wide range of audiences e.g. graphs and data, very clear tips, extract in multiple formats

  • Workaround for the technical challenges

    • A combination use of R packages such as flexdashboard, crosstalk, plotly, DT and flextable
    • Minimal amount of Javascript and CSS to create an “illusion” of dependency dropdown 😵
